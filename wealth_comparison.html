CFD total wealth = active lending capital + all cash extracted to live on (the "spend half" income). House equity = home value at 2%/yr appreciation minus remaining mortgage balance (mortgage pays down ~$250–350/mo principal early years, increasing slightly over time). House scenario assumes $1,566/mo shortfall (mortgage − rent − prop mgmt) is covered from other income. CFD assumes $270K deployed on day 1 at 16% interest-only, compounding semi-annually as reinvested half is added to principal each year. Capital can always be redeployed or returned — it's liquid in a way house equity is not.
